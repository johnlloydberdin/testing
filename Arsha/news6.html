GOV AMANTE CHALLENGES LGUS TO DEVELOP STRATEGIC PLAN IN CRISIS, DRR MANAGEMENT IN AGUSAN DEL NORTE

Agusan del Norte Provincial Governor Ma. Angelica Rosedell M. Amante, encouraged and challenged each Local Government Unit in Agusan del Norte to make a right and develop an in-depth plan in regard to the Crisis and Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR) Management with the help of pieces of training and seminars by the Provincial Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (PDRRMO) for the implementation in their respective areas.

According to Gov. Amante, in her message during the Crisis and DRR Management: Co-design Workshop for the Development of Local Risk and Resilience Index for the Province of Agusan del Norte earlier today, November 21 at the E&G Convention Center, Cabadbaran City, there are some areas in Agusan del Norte where facilities and establishments were built along frontline and danger zone areas.

"I have my first clients earlier, who have been displaced because of the rising tide nga dili na mapugngan. There were fisherfolks and we have to relocate them. Problems like this can be prevented if there is tama nga planning in our municipalities" the governor said. â€œPero kung dili ta mag plano daan in an anticipation of the danger zones in our areas, your resources will be eaten up by all of these problems" she added. Gov. Amante emphasized the importance of the said training and seminar on how it can help the LGUs to create a plan and implement it in their respective areas.

The 2-day seminar and workshop covered the following discussions: a) Assessing Resilience-What does it mean for Philippine LGUs?; b) Exposing Exposure and Making Sense of Sensitivity; c) Measuring Risk Reduction Capacity and Recovery Potential; and d) All Together Now: Coming Up with a Composition Index. The workshop it covers 1) Validating Initial List if Resilience Indicators; 2) Testing the Local Resilience Index for Agusan del Norte LGUs.

Present at the said event were Lead Facilitator Dr. Kristoffer Berse, DILG Provincial Director Ellen Chua, Board Member Nieva Famador, President of Mayors League Zenaida Lim, and among others.

[By: Karen Kie Decamotan]
